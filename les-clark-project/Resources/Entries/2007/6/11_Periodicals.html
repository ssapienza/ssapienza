Periodicals

 

Below is a bibliography of periodical resources about all kinds of animation subjects, from technical subjects (see the American Cinematographer, Millimeter and Journal of SMPTE articles) to critical reviews in Film Journal, New York Times and the LA Times.

 

Periodical Resources

Monday, June 11, 2007

Periodicals – Animation (General):


Gilbert Seldes, “No Art, Mr. Disney?” Esquire (September 1937)

Seldes says that The Band Concert is Disney’s single greatest work.

Find in a library:

http://www.worldcatlibraries.org/oclc/1568235



Al Hirschfield, “an Artists Contests Mr. Disney,” New York Times (January 30, 1938). 

Harsh criticism of Snow White’s animation by the famous caricaturist.

Find in a library:

http://www.worldcatlibraries.org/oclc/1645522

Most libraries have a subscription to ProQuest, an online database through which you can search older issues of the Times online.



Wililam Stull, “Three hundred men: and Walt Disney”

American Cinematographer 19 (February 1938): 48-50

Written at the time of Snow White’s production, this cinematography trade magazine article covers many aspects of animation filming technology as well as the Multiplane camera.

Find in a library:

http://www.worldcatlibraries.org/oclc/1479664

American Cinematographer has featured many animation articles over the years.  You can find many back issues of their magazine in their online archives: http://www.theasc.com/magazine/archive.htm (some are free and some require purchase).



Walt Disney, “Growing Pains,” Journal of SMPTE (January 1941)

Disney’s personal account of studio issues and challenges up through Fantasia.

Find in a library:

http://www.worldcatlibraries.org/oclc/7000262

Like American Cinematographer, the SMPTE magazine frequently concerns itself with technical issues related to animation.  The organization keeps indexes of every issue since 1916 here: http://www.smpte.org/resources/smpte_journal/journal_index



Carl Fallberg, "Animated Film Technique" (series of nine articles)

American Cinematographer (July 1958 through March 1959)

Find in a library:

http://www.worldcatlibraries.org/oclc/1479664



Jonathan Rosenbaum, Dream Masters I: Walt Disney,” Film Comment (January-February 1975) Aesthetic assessment of Disney’s output, including style and overall vision.

Find in a library:

http://www.worldcatlibraries.org/oclc/2266507



David Smith, “The Sorcerer’s Apprentice” Millimeter (February 1976)

Special Animation issue of the magazine contains an article by Smith of the Disney Archives, which talks in depth about Sorcerer’s Apprentice.

Find in a library:

http://www.worldcatlibraries.org/oclc/60624086

Millimeter is yet another article that continuously covers issues related to animation  Although their past issues are not indexed online, they are available online in most public libraries through Lexis Nexis.



William Moritz, “The Incontrovertibly Enduring Impact of Mickey Mouse”

New York Times (December 3, 1977)

Find in a library:

http://www.worldcatlibraries.org/oclc/1645522



Stephen Jay Gould, “Mickey Mouse Meets Konrad Lorenz,” Natural History (May 1979) Cover story, the development of Mickey, with comment from animal behaviorists.

Find in a library:

http://www.worldcatlibraries.org/oclc/1759475



Mike Barrier, “Building a Better Mouse,” Funnyworld 20 (Summer 1979)

Find in a library:

http://www.worldcatlibraries.org/oclc/3067704

Most animators have back issues of this magazine stacked up and hoarded for their value as resources.  I highly suggest browsing as many issues as you can. Several Funnyworld articles are also posted here on Michael Barrier’s website.



Jack Kinney, “Bambi and the Goof,” Funnyworld 21 (Fall 1979) Anecdotal recollections of the older days of the studio, by a former shorts director.

Find in a library:

http://www.worldcatlibraries.org/oclc/3067704



David Heuring, and George Turner, "Disney's Fantasia: Yesterday and Today"

American Cinematographer (February 1991)

Find in a library:

http://www.worldcatlibraries.org/oclc/1479664



Chris Riemenschneider, "How a 67-year-old mouse became a corporate big-shot"

Los Angeles Times 114 (September 24, 1995)

Find in a library:

http://www.worldcatlibraries.org/oclc/3638237

Like the New York Times, you can search older issues of the LA Times online through ProQuest, available at most public and academic libraries.



C. Panzer, "Uncle Walt's Cabinet of Artistic Delights."

Animation Magazine 20, no. 12 (December 2006)

Find in a library:

http://www.worldcatlibraries.org/oclc/18794679



Victoria Oxberry, "'I Didn't Mean to Frighten You': The Disney Gothic."

Film Journal 1, no. 13 (Winter 2006)

Find in a library:

http://www.worldcatlibraries.org/oclc/49732049
