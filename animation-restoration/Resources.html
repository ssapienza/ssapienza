Web and Print Resources

Articles and Books:



Charles Solomon, Enchanted Drawings: The History of Animation

(New York: Random House, 1989)

Find in a library: http://www.worldcatlibraries.org/oclc/19811383&referer=brief_results


Frank Thomas and Ollie Johnston, The Illusion of Life: Disney Animation

(New York: Abbeville Press, 1981)

Find in a library: http://www.worldcatlibraries.org/oclc/7900428&referer=brief_results


Leonard Maltin, Of mice and magic: a history of American animated cartoons

(New York: New American Library, 1980)

Find in a library: http://www.worldcatlibraries.org/oclc/6331220&referer=brief_results


Robert D. Field, The Art of Walt Disney   

(New York: The Macmillan Co., 1942)

Concentrates on the traditionally artistic aspects of Disney animation, with a focus on the early features and shorts from the 1940’s and 1950’s.

Find in a library: http://www.worldcatlibraries.org/oclc/1192053&referer=brief_results


Dave Smith, "The Walt Disney Archives: It All Started With a Mouse." Historical Journal of Film, Television and Radio 16, no. 1 (March 1996): 13-19.

Articles and Books:


Scott MacQueen, "Snow White and the Seven Dwarves: Epic Animation Restored"

The Perfect Vision 6, no. 22: 26-31.

Includes color frame blow-ups comparing image quality of an original nitrate dye-transfer print, a 1987 version restored by YCM Labs, and the 1993 digital restoration performed on Kodak's CINEON workstation.


Bob Fisher, “Off to Work We Go: The Digital Restoration of Snow White”

American Cinematographer 74 (September 1993):  46-54

Case Study on the digital restoration of Snow White.


Robert Harris, “Getting It Right: Robert Harris on Color Restoration” Film History 12 (3): 393-409
